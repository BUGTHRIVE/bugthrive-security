# Safe Harbor

BugThrive supports responsible, good-faith security research that helps improve the security of our systems, services, users, and customers.

This Safe Harbor applies to security research conducted in accordance with the BugThrive Vulnerability Disclosure Policy, Security Policy, Authorized Scope, Rules of Engagement, and applicable law.

---

## Our Commitment

When a researcher conducts security research in good faith and complies with BugThrive’s published security policies, BugThrive will consider that research authorized under this Vulnerability Disclosure Program.

BugThrive will not initiate or support legal action against a researcher solely for security research conducted in good-faith compliance with these policies.

BugThrive will work with researchers who:

- Test only authorized, in-scope assets
- Follow the Rules of Engagement
- Use non-destructive and minimally invasive testing methods
- Avoid harm to BugThrive, its users, customers, employees, systems, services, and data
- Stop testing when sufficient evidence has been obtained
- Stop testing immediately when sensitive information is encountered
- Promptly and privately report vulnerabilities
- Provide accurate information and reasonable assistance during investigation
- Keep vulnerability information confidential
- Allow BugThrive an opportunity to investigate and remediate the issue
- Obtain confirmation from BugThrive before public disclosure
- Comply with applicable laws

---

## Good-Faith Research

BugThrive generally considers research to be conducted in good faith when the researcher:

- Acts with the purpose of identifying and responsibly reporting a security vulnerability
- Makes a reasonable effort to avoid privacy violations, service disruption, data loss, and harm
- Tests only assets authorized under the [Authorized Scope](SCOPE.md)
- Uses only the level of access necessary to demonstrate the vulnerability
- Does not exploit the vulnerability beyond what is reasonably necessary for validation
- Uses researcher-owned accounts and data whenever possible
- Does not access or modify another user’s account or data
- Does not retain, transfer, sell, misuse, or publicly disclose information obtained during testing
- Reports the vulnerability through an approved private reporting channel
- Cooperates reasonably with BugThrive during validation and remediation
- Follows coordinated disclosure requirements
- Complies with the [Rules of Engagement](RULES-OF-ENGAGEMENT.md)

---

## Research BugThrive Does Not Intend to Pursue

When conducted in full compliance with this policy, BugThrive does not intend to pursue claims against researchers solely for:

- Good-faith security testing of authorized, in-scope BugThrive assets
- Circumvention of technical controls when reasonably necessary to validate an in-scope vulnerability
- Limited interaction with BugThrive systems when necessary to demonstrate a security issue
- Accidental, minimal, and promptly reported access that occurs during otherwise compliant research

This statement does not authorize unrestricted access, exploitation, data collection, service disruption, or activities prohibited by law or BugThrive policy.

---

## Sensitive Information

If a researcher unintentionally encounters sensitive information, the researcher must:

1. Stop testing immediately.
2. Avoid further access.
3. Avoid accessing additional records.
4. Avoid copying, downloading, retaining, modifying, deleting, transferring, or disclosing the information.
5. Notify BugThrive promptly.
6. Include only the minimum evidence necessary to explain the vulnerability.
7. Follow BugThrive’s reasonable instructions regarding secure handling or deletion.

Accidental access does not automatically remove safe-harbor consideration when the researcher acts responsibly, minimizes exposure, stops testing, and promptly reports the issue.

Intentional or unnecessary access to sensitive information is not protected.

---

## Activities Not Covered

Safe Harbor does not apply to:

- Testing assets outside the authorized scope
- Testing unofficial, unsupported, deprecated, unauthorized, or “Coming Soon” deployments
- Testing third-party systems or services without authorization
- Intentional harm
- Service disruption
- Denial-of-service or distributed denial-of-service testing
- Stress testing, load testing, or resource exhaustion
- Aggressive or disruptive automated scanning
- Social engineering or phishing
- Physical attacks
- Credential stuffing, password spraying, or brute-force attacks
- Use of credentials obtained from unauthorized sources
- Malware, ransomware, spyware, destructive payloads, or persistent malicious code
- Establishing persistence or installing backdoors
- Unauthorized lateral movement
- Access beyond what is minimally necessary to demonstrate a vulnerability
- Intentional access to another user’s account or information
- Privacy violations
- Data theft, copying, retention, modification, destruction, sale, transfer, or disclosure
- Extortion, threats, coercion, or demands
- Exploitation for personal, commercial, financial, competitive, or malicious advantage
- Public disclosure without prior confirmation from BugThrive
- Misrepresentation of research activities or findings
- Activities that violate applicable law
- Any activity prohibited by BugThrive’s Security Policy or Rules of Engagement

---

## Third-Party Systems

This Safe Harbor applies only to systems and assets owned or controlled by BugThrive for which BugThrive has authority to permit security testing.

BugThrive cannot authorize testing of third-party systems and cannot provide protection from actions taken by independent third parties.

The presence of third-party technology, infrastructure, integrations, applications, or services on a BugThrive domain does not automatically make those systems eligible for testing.

Researchers are responsible for obtaining authorization from third parties where required.

---

## Third-Party Legal Action

If a third party initiates legal action against a researcher for activities conducted in good-faith compliance with BugThrive’s published security policies, BugThrive may, where appropriate and legally permitted, clarify that the research was conducted under this Vulnerability Disclosure Program.

BugThrive cannot bind third parties, control their actions, guarantee their response, or provide legal representation.

---

## Coordinated Disclosure Requirement

Safe-harbor consideration requires researchers to keep vulnerability information confidential until BugThrive confirms that public disclosure may proceed.

Researchers must not publicly disclose vulnerability details, proofs of concept, screenshots, videos, exploitation methods, private communications, investigation details, or remediation information without prior confirmation from BugThrive.

Submission, acknowledgement, validation, remediation, or closure of a report does not automatically grant permission for public disclosure.

---

## No Authorization to Violate Law

This Safe Harbor does not authorize any activity prohibited by applicable law.

Researchers are responsible for complying with applicable legal requirements and respecting the rights, privacy, property, and systems of others.

Nothing in this policy grants ownership rights, access rights beyond authorized testing, permission to use BugThrive intellectual property, or permission to access third-party systems.

---

## No Guarantee of Reward

Safe-harbor consideration is independent of recognition or reward.

BugThrive may provide a Letter of Appreciation for validated reports, subject to its review and policies.

Submission or validation of a report does not create an entitlement to:

- Financial compensation
- A monetary bounty
- Employment
- Contractual engagement
- Public recognition
- Any other reward or benefit

---

## Policy Interpretation

BugThrive retains responsibility for determining whether research was conducted in good faith and in accordance with its published policies.

BugThrive may consider:

- The researcher’s intent
- The scope and nature of testing
- The methods used
- The amount of access obtained
- Whether harm occurred
- Whether testing stopped when appropriate
- How sensitive information was handled
- The quality and timeliness of the report
- The researcher’s cooperation
- Compliance with coordinated disclosure requirements

BugThrive will aim to evaluate the complete circumstances fairly and reasonably.

---

## Questions and Clarification

If you are uncertain whether planned research is authorized or covered by this Safe Harbor, contact BugThrive before proceeding:

`compliance@bugthrive.com`

Do not assume authorization when the scope or permitted activity is unclear.
